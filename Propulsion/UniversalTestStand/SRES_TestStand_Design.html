05 — DATA ACQUISITION SYSTEM
WHAT IT MEASURES
Thrust — S-type load cell → HX711 → Arduino
Chamber pressure — analog transducer → Arduino ADC
Time — Arduino micros() at 100 Hz loop
All logged to SD card + streamed to laptop
WHAT YOU GET OUT
Thrust curve — F vs t graph, total impulse by integration
Pc vs t — chamber pressure trace
Isp — calculated post-fire from thrust + known mass flow
Regression rate — derived from pre/post grain mass + burn time
06 — ASSEMBLY SEQUENCE
01
Cut and weld the base frame
Cut 50×50×5 MS angle into: 2× 800mm rails, 4× 300mm crossmembers. Weld into a flat rectangular ladder frame. Weld anchor foot plates (100×100×8mm) at all four corners. Drill M12 anchor holes through each foot. This is the only welding job — take the cut list to your local welder.
02
Weld the reaction wall
Weld the 200×200×10mm plate vertically at the nose end of the frame, centred on the thrust axis. This wall takes the full 5,000 N reaction force through the load cell. Weld a load cell mounting boss (M16 threaded rod, 30mm long) centred on the wall face.
⚠ This weld is the most structurally critical. Full penetration weld, both sides. No tack welds.
03
Install the load cell inline
Thread the S-type load cell onto the reaction wall boss. Thread a motor thrust plate (50×50×8mm, M16 hole) onto the other end of the load cell. The thrust force path is: motor nose → thrust plate → load cell → reaction wall → ground. The load cell reads thrust directly.
04
Weld motor support uprights
Weld two upright plates (150×200×8mm) vertically onto the base rails, spaced to match your motor length. Drill four M8 holes in each upright to accept the clamp collar bolts. These uprights carry the radial loads only — they do not carry thrust.
05
Install interchangeable clamp collars
Bolt the 50mm clamp collar onto the uprights for first test campaign. When switching to 75mm motors, unbolt and swap collars in under 5 minutes. The collar grips the motor body radially — it does not restrain the motor axially. The motor nose pushes against the thrust plate and the load cell reads that force.
06
Wire the DAQ system
Connect load cell → HX711 → Arduino digital pins. Connect pressure transducer analog output → Arduino A0. Wire SD card module via SPI. Upload the logging sketch (100 Hz loop, CSV output). Run a 10m USB cable or HC-12 wireless pair to the laptop behind the berm. Test with known weights before first fire.
07
Install fluid system (hybrid only)
Route SS tubing from N₂O cylinder → solenoid valve → injector port on motor. Install pressure relief valve between cylinder and solenoid, set to 60 bar. The solenoid valve is controlled by a relay on the Arduino — this is your remote oxidiser control. Never open the N₂O cylinder manually during a fire.
⚠ All fittings rated to minimum 100 bar. No plastic or rubber push-fit fittings in the high-pressure line.
08
Anchor, verify, cold-flow test
Anchor the stand to ground with M12 bolts or sandbag weights. Before any hot fire, do a cold-flow test — open the N₂O valve with no ignition and verify flow, check for leaks at every fitting, confirm the pressure trace logs correctly. Only after a clean cold-flow test do you proceed to a hot fire.
07 — SAFETY RULES
Minimum 30 metres between operator and stand during any hot or cold-flow test. No exceptions.
All ignition and valve commands via remote control only. Nobody approaches the stand until N₂O cylinder is fully closed and 5 minutes have elapsed.
Pressure relief valve must be installed and tested before every session. Never defeat or bypass it.
Fire extinguisher (CO₂ type, minimum 2 kg) positioned 15m downrange, accessible from the operator position.
All personnel wear safety glasses. At least one person designated as safety officer per session — their call is final.
Natural earthen berm or concrete block between operator and stand whenever possible. Stand oriented so exhaust fires away from operator and any structures.
No test fire without at least two people present. No solo testing. Ever.
Post-fire inspection before approaching stand: confirm N₂O cylinder valve closed, wait 5 minutes, approach from the side — never from nozzle or nose direction.
