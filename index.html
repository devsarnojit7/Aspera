RECOMMENDED PATH FOR ASPERA
Phase 1 — Now: Master KNDX solid motors. Static test on a thrust stand. Validate Kn calculations, chamber pressure, and nozzle geometry. This is your propulsion school.

Phase 2 — Pre-Aspera: Develop an N₂O / HTPB hybrid motor targeting M-class total impulse (~10,000–15,000 Ns). This gives you ~245 s Isp — nearly twice the sugar propellants — and is what gets you to 7 km with a practical rocket mass. N₂O is obtainable in India (used in food/industrial applications). HTPB requires sourcing but is available from chemical suppliers.

Why not APCP: Ammonium perchlorate is a controlled explosive precursor in India under the Explosives Act. Procurement without DRDO/institutional affiliation is not realistic.

Why not liquid for Aspera: Liquid engines require injector design, feed system, combustion chamber cooling, and cryogenic or pressurised propellant handling — each of which is a year-long project on its own. Liquid is a post-Aspera SRES milestone, not the vehicle for it.
